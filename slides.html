DAY 1 Sat, May 23

Arrive in Tbilisi

Tbilisi — Old Town
Arrival day — no driving
  • Arrive at Tbilisi airport, pick up rental car & check into Old Town hotel
  • Evening stroll through the narrow streets of Abanotubani — brick-domed bathhouses, carved wooden balconies, the Mtkvari river
  • Dinner at a traditional Old Town restaurant — try khinkali and churchkhela
Tip: Leave the car parked at the hotel — you won't need it for the first 4 days in Tbilisi.
Jvari Monastery, Mtskheta • Wikimedia Commons
DAY 2 Sun, May 24

Mtskheta & Uplistsikhe

Mtskheta & Uplistsikhe — day trip from Tbilisi
~2.5h total driving
  • Drive to Mtskheta (~30 min) — Georgia's ancient capital at the confluence of two rivers
  • Jvari Monastery (6th century) — hilltop with sweeping views where the Mtkvari and Aragvi rivers meet below
  • Svetitskhoveli Cathedral — one of the most sacred sites in Georgia, alleged burial site of Christ's robe
  • Continue to Uplistsikhe (~1h) — a 3,000-year-old cave city carved into rock, with wine cellars, theatres, and a basilica
Narikala Fortress, Tbilisi • Wikimedia Commons
DAY 3 Mon, May 25

Tbilisi Deep Dive

Tbilisi — Old Town & city centre
Walking day — no driving
  • Climb to Narikala Fortress for the best panoramic view of the city — Old Town rooftops, the river, and mountains beyond
  • Book a private room at Chreli Abano for a traditional sulfur bath — one of Tbilisi's most unique experiences
  • Browse the Dry Bridge Market — Soviet-era curiosities, antiques, paintings, and memorabilia spread across a bridge
  • Pre-Independence Day buzz building across the city — enjoy the atmosphere
Rustaveli Avenue, Tbilisi • Wikimedia Commons
DAY 4 Tue, May 26

Independence Day

Tbilisi — Rustaveli Avenue & Rike Park
No driving — city on foot
  • Military parade along Rustaveli Avenue from ~10 AM — troops, armoured vehicles, aircraft flypasts
  • Rike Park fills with open-air concerts, folk dancing, and food stalls throughout the afternoon
  • Fireworks in the evening — best viewed from near Metekhi Church or Rike Park
May 26: Georgia's most important national holiday, celebrating independence declared in 1918. Warm, festive, and proudly celebrated. Most of Old Town will be pedestrianized.

DAY 5 Wed, May 27

Road Trip Begins: Rabati & Vardzia

Tbilisi → Akhaltsikhe → Vardzia
~5h driving (with stops)
  • Drive southwest through the heartland — dramatic mountain scenery unfolds
  • Rabati Castle in Akhaltsikhe — lunch & 2-hour explore of the fortress blending Orthodox and Islamic architecture
  • Quick stop at Khertvisi Fortress — dramatic roadside medieval castle above a gorge
  • Arrive at Vardzia area by early evening

DAY 6 Thu, May 28

Vardzia Cave City

Vardzia — Samtskhe-Javakheti
Exploration day — minimal driving
  • Full morning exploring Vardzia — 640 surviving cave dwellings carved into a sheer cliff face
  • 12th-century frescoed cave church with original paintings still visible inside
  • Walk through tunnels and stairways connecting multiple levels — extraordinary cliffside views
  • Afternoon relaxing by the river below the caves
Book ahead: Accommodation near Vardzia is very limited. Reserve early.

DAY 7 Fri, May 29

Vardzia to Batumi

Vardzia → Batumi (Black Sea)
~2.5h driving
  • Easy drive west to the Black Sea coast — landscape shifts from mountains to subtropical
  • Arrive in Batumi — a lively resort city with palm-lined boulevards, eclectic architecture, and a fun energy
  • Afternoon on the seafront promenade — cafes, sculptures, sea breeze
  • Settle into the hotel and enjoy a first evening by the sea

DAY 8 Sat, May 30

Full Day in Batumi

Batumi — Black Sea coast
Beach day — no driving
  • Morning at the Batumi Botanical Garden — one of the best in the region, with sweeping sea views and subtropical plants
  • Afternoon swimming in the Black Sea — your only real beach day on the whole trip
  • Explore the eclectic Old Town architecture — mix of Art Nouveau, Ottoman, and Soviet

DAY 9 Sun, May 31

Into the Mountains: Svaneti

Batumi → Mestia (Svaneti)
~3.5–4h driving
  • Drive north via Zugdidi — the last 60km to Mestia is on a slow mountain road where the 4x4 earns its keep
  • Arrive in Mestia — medieval defensive towers scattered across hillsides, snow-capped peaks all around
  • Noticeably cooler air than the coast — a different world
  • Settle in and explore the village on foot

DAY 10 Mon, June 1

Mestia: Museum & Glacier Hike

Mestia, Svaneti
Hiking day — minimal driving
  • Visit the Svaneti Museum of History and Ethnography — understand the extraordinary isolated culture of this region
  • Hike to Chalaadi Glacier — flat, easy 5km trail through forest ending at the snout of an active glacier
  • Good acclimatization before the more demanding Ushguli day tomorrow
Ushguli & Mt. Shkhara • Wikimedia Commons
DAY 11 Tue, June 2

Ushguli — Europe's Highest Village

Mestia → Ushguli → Mestia (day trip)
~3–4h driving (rough mountain road)
  • Ushguli at 2,200m — one of the highest continuously inhabited settlements in Europe
  • Cluster of medieval Svan towers against the backdrop of Mt. Shkhara (Georgia's highest peak, 5,193m)
  • Road takes 1.5–2h each way — 4x4 mandatory, not optional
  • Spend 4–5 hours wandering freely — one of the trip's absolute highlights
4x4 required: The Ushguli road is rough and unpaved. Do not attempt in a standard car.

DAY 12 Wed, June 3

Svaneti → Kutaisi → Tbilisi

Mestia → Kutaisi → Tbilisi
~6.5h driving (longest day)
  • Leave Mestia by 8 AM for the long transit day
  • Stop in Kutaisi for Gelati Monastery — 12th-century UNESCO site built by King David the Builder
  • Stunning mosaics and hilltop setting above a forested valley
  • Push on to Tbilisi (~3h from Kutaisi) — evening rest before the northern leg
Longest drive: ~6.5h total. Leave early and break it up with the Gelati stop.

DAY 13 Thu, June 4

The Military Highway to Kazbegi

Tbilisi → Ananuri → Kazbegi (Stepantsminda)
~3h driving (with stops)
  • Head north on the legendary Georgian Military Highway
  • Stop at Ananuri Fortress perched above the Zhinvali Reservoir (1h)
  • Gudauri panoramic viewpoint — dramatic mountain pass views
  • Drive up to Gergeti Trinity Church — 14th-century monastery at 2,170m with Mt. Kazbegi rising behind
Golden hour: Afternoon light at Gergeti is exceptional — plan to arrive by 3–4 PM.

DAY 14 Fri, June 5

Kazbegi: Truso Gorge & Dariali

Kazbegi — Stepantsminda area
~2h driving (mountain roads)
  • Dariali Monastery right on the Russian border — framed by near-vertical cliff walls, one of the most dramatic settings in Georgia
  • Truso Gorge (4x4 required) — broad mountain valley with mineral hot springs and free-roaming horses
  • Ruins of Zakagori Fortress at the end of the valley
  • Alternative: Gergeti glacier day-hike for more mountain time

DAY 15 Sat, June 6

Kakheti Wine Country

Kazbegi → Kakheti (via Tbilisi)
~3.5h driving
  • Drive south through Tbilisi and east into Kakheti — Georgia's premier wine region
  • Afternoon wine tasting at a winery near Telavi — look for traditional qvevri (underground clay vessel) winemaking
  • Georgia's 8,000-year winemaking tradition is UNESCO-listed — taste it at the source
  • Night in Telavi or Sighnaghi

DAY 16 Sun, June 7

Sighnaghi & Departure

Sighnaghi → Tbilisi Airport
~2h driving to airport
  • Final morning in Sighnaghi — Georgia's prettiest walled village perched on a hill overlooking the Alazani Valley
  • Walk the cobblestone streets and old city walls for one last view
  • One last wine stop before the road
  • 2h drive back to Tbilisi airport — return car & depart
Final count: 16 days, ~1,600 km, one clean clockwise loop through the heart of Georgia.
