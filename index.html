Three distinct ways to experience Georgia's extraordinary diversity

Easy Difficulty 📅 14 Days 📍 6 Stops 💰 $900 – $1,400

Classic Georgia

"The essential Georgian experience — capital to mountains to wine country"

The perfect first-time itinerary. Start in cosmopolitan Tbilisi, make a half-day pilgrimage to ancient Mtskheta, head west to Kutaisi and its medieval UNESCO monasteries, then ascend to the dramatic Kazbegi highlands before winding down through the vine-draped Kakheti region. A beautifully balanced mix of cities, culture, mountains, and wine.

UNESCO SitesWine RegionMountainsOld TownMonasteriesDay Hikes
3 Nights

Tbilisi

Capital City

The vibrant capital where Persian-influenced balconies hang over sulfuric bath steam and modern wine bars thrive in the Old Town.

  • Narikala Fortress & Abanotubani baths
  • Old Town stroll & Georgian cuisine
  • Dry Bridge antique market
Day Trip

Mtskheta

Ancient Capital — UNESCO

Georgia's spiritual heart where the Mtkvari and Aragvi rivers meet beneath centuries-old fortified churches.

  • Svetitskhoveli Cathedral (11th century)
  • Jvari Monastery — panoramic river views
  • Samtavro Nunnery
2 Nights

Kutaisi

Western Georgia

Georgia's ancient second city, gateway to lush forests, dramatic cave systems, and the medieval Gelati Monastery.

  • Gelati Monastery — UNESCO World Heritage
  • Prometheus Cave & Sataplia Nature Reserve
  • Bagrati Cathedral ruins
3 Nights

Kazbegi (Stepantsminda)

Greater Caucasus

The iconic mountain town beneath Mt. Kazbek (5,047 m), crowned by the legendary Gergeti Trinity Church perched at 2,170 m.

  • Gergeti Trinity Church hike (2–3 hrs)
  • Georgian Military Highway & Ananuri Fortress
  • Truso Valley gorge walk
2 Nights

Sighnaghi

Kakheti Wine Region

Georgia's "City of Love" — a charming walled hilltop town surrounded by vineyards above the Alazani Valley plains.

  • 18th-century city walls & watchtowers
  • Bodbe Monastery and nunnery
  • Local wine cellars & qvevri tasting
1 Night

Telavi

Heart of Kakheti

Capital of the Kakheti wine region, surrounded by medieval fortresses and Georgia's most celebrated wineries.

  • Batonis Tsikhe fortress
  • Alaverdi Cathedral (11th century)
  • Family winery visits & Nekresi Monastery

Adventure & Nature

"For trekkers and wilderness lovers — Georgia's raw, untamed highlands"

Georgia's most spectacular backcountry in 14 days. After one night in Tbilisi you head straight to Kazbegi for high-altitude acclimatisation, then journey to mystical Svaneti — the land of medieval towers — before trekking to remote Ushguli, one of Europe's highest inhabited settlements at 2,200 m. For physically fit, well-prepared travellers only.

Multi-day TrekkingAlpine SceneryRemote VillagesMedieval TowersUNESCO SvanetiGlaciers
1 Night

Tbilisi

Capital — Arrival & Gear Up

Arrive, rest, and stock up on trail supplies. Book onward transport and a mountain guide for Kazbegi.

  • Gear check & supply run
  • Quick Old Town orientation walk
  • Pre-trek dinner at Fabrika district
3 Nights

Kazbegi (Stepantsminda)

Greater Caucasus — 1,700 m

Acclimatise at altitude before tackling Svaneti. The Gergeti glacier trek, Truso Valley gorge, and Dariali Canyon await.

  • Gergeti Trinity Church hike
  • Truso Valley mineral springs gorge
  • Optional Gergeti Glacier full-day trek
4 Nights

Mestia / Svaneti

Upper Svaneti — UNESCO

The Svaneti highlands capital, flanked by 9th–12th century defensive towers and surrounded by 4,000 m peaks.

  • Koruldi Lakes day trek (6–7 hrs)
  • Chalaadi Glacier hike (4–5 hrs)
  • Svan towers & local museum
3 Nights

Ushguli

Europe's Highest Village — 2,200 m

Reached by a 2–3 day multi-stage trek from Mestia, Ushguli offers views of Mt. Shkhara (5,193 m).

  • Mestia–Ushguli multi-day trek (58 km)
  • Shkhara glacier viewpoint
  • Lamaria Church ruins & village walks

A relaxed finish in Georgia's oldest city — lush gorges, underground caves, ancient manuscripts, and excellent local food.

Practical Travel Tips

Everything you need to know before you go

✈️

Getting There

Tbilisi International Airport (TBS) has direct flights from most European hubs, Istanbul, Dubai, and Yerevan. Low-cost airlines like Wizzair and Ryanair operate seasonal routes.

🛂

Visa

Citizens of 95+ countries including EU, US, UK, Canada, and Australia receive a free 1-year visa on arrival. No advance booking needed — just show your passport.

💴

Currency

Georgian Lari (GEL). 1 USD ≈ 2.7 GEL. ATMs are widespread in cities; rural areas are cash-only. Georgia is extremely affordable by European standards.

🚐

Getting Around

Marshrutkas (shared minibuses) connect all major towns cheaply. Bolt app works great in Tbilisi. Rent a car for Kakheti freedom. 4WD needed for Svaneti.

🍷

Food & Wine

Try khinkali (dumplings), khachapuri (cheese bread), churchkhela, and natural orange wine from 8,000-year-old qvevri clay vessels. Georgia is the cradle of wine.

📅

Best Time to Visit

May–June and September–October are ideal. Summer is fine but hot in cities. Mountain passes close November to May. Kakheti harvest is late September.

📶

Connectivity

Buy a Magti or Geocell SIM at the airport for ~$5 with generous 4G data. Wi-Fi is reliable in guesthouses and cafes. Mountain regions have limited signal.

🙏

Culture & Etiquette

Cover shoulders and knees in churches. Always accept a supra feast invitation. Georgians are famously hospitable — a kind word goes a very long way.
